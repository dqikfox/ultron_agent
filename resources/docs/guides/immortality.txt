HeLa refers to a cell line derived from Henrietta Lacks, an African American woman who died of cervical cancer in 1951. Her cells, named HeLa (first two letters of her first and last name), were unique because they could reproduce indefinitely in a laboratory setting, making them the first human cell line to achieve this. This "immortality" has made HeLa cells invaluable in biomedical research, contributing to advancements in various fields
